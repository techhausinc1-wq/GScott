How to use GSW HQ

G. Scott Waddell Co. — Modern Line Furniture's manufacturer's rep into Texas + surrounding-state hospitality. This tool runs your day.

Scout

Pick a state, ZIP, and radius. Check the hospitality verticals you want to target (country clubs, golf, restaurants, hotels, bowling, resorts, casinos, senior living, etc.). Tap Run Scout Agent. The AI runs web searches and returns 20-30 real venues with phone, address, website, score, and the angle to call with. Use Quick Discover presets for common combinations (Dallas country clubs, Houston hospitality, etc.).

Lookup

When you hear of a company by name — at an event, from a friend, in a referral — go to the Lookup tab. Type the company name and optional location. In 30-90 seconds you get a full buyer dossier: who to call, real contact info, estimated revenue, what to say tailored to Modern Line's US-made advantage, an opening line, and red flags to avoid.

Today / Called

Today is your daily call queue. Build it from Prospects or Scout results, then work through each card. Each card shows the pitch, the SKU recommendations, the buyer angle, and one-tap outcomes (Got DM, Left VM, Meeting Set, Drop By, Order Closed). Outcomes auto-log into Called for end-of-day review and Sheets sync.

Commissions

When you tap Order Closed in Today, a modal asks for the order total. Commission is calculated automatically and lands here as Pending. Master users (Ivan or Vladimir) mark commissions Earned (when the manufacturer payment lands) and Paid (when your check clears). Per-user rates set in Admin.

Scan Card

At an event, after a meeting, anywhere you collect a business card — tap Scan Card in the header, take a photo, and the AI extracts name, title, company, phone, email, address. You confirm, edit anything wrong, and save into Prospects + optionally Today.

Why hospitality buyers go US-made through G. Scott + Modern Line
  • Lead time: 4–8 weeks vs 12–20+ weeks offshore. Critical when opening dates are real.
  • Custom upholstery / COM: Customer's Own Material accepted. Offshore typically can't.
  • Small minimums: 5–12 piece custom runs. Offshore requires 50+ MOQ.
  • Zero tariff exposure: US-made avoids the 25%+ tariffs on Chinese furniture.
  • On-site repair: G. Scott does touch-up and repair in Texas. Offshore parts are typically unavailable — "replace not repair."
  • Reorders: Match an exact piece years later for incremental refreshes (matters for 200-room hotel resets).
  • Fire code: Cal-117 and NFPA 260 compliance baked in. Offshore often requires re-certification.
  • Same-region delivery + install: G. Scott handles delivery and installation in TX, OK, AR, LA, NM, KS.
